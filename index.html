Test Based Assesments

Postural stability test

During the test, the patient's ability to maintain their balance is evaluated, and any deviations from the center are assessed. In this test, a lower score is considered more desirable than a higher score, as it indicates better postural stability.

Know More

Limit of Stability Test

The patient's Limits of Stability is measured as the maximum angle they can sway from the center in different directions . The sway angles (°) derived from the position of the patient's center of gravity (COG) is displayed on the screen.

Know More

Weight distribution

Weight distribution analysis involves examining the distribution of weight in both the anterior-posterior and medial-lateral directions.

Know More

Ground reaction force

This mode helps to assess GRF during the gait. Reba is placed in the gait path and the patient is requested to place one foot at a time and ground reaction force distribution will be provided for left and right foot after the assessment.

Know More

mCTSIB

The Clinical Test of Sensory Interaction and Balance (CTSIB) is a standardized test for balance assessment on a static surface. The test provides a generalized assessment of how well a patient can integrate various sensory conditions during a balance test. The test accounts for how well the patient compensates when one or more of those sensory conditions are compromised.

Know More

BESS test

During a BESS test, balance tasks are performed on both a level surface and a foam pad. The Reba BESS test is an enhanced version of the more traditional BESS test. In a traditional BESS test, scoring is time and error-based. With the Reba's force platform technology, time and error counting is replaced with an objective quantification of sway.

Know More

Fall Risk Test

The Reba System Fall Risk test allows identification of potential fall candidates. Test results can compared to baseline parameters. Higher scores suggest further assessment for lower extremity strength, proprioception, and vestibular or visual deficiencies.
